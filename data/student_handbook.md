# GENERAL INFORMATION

## "Why Choose President University?"

President University is Indonesia’s premier university with the highest enrolment of full-time international students. Below are the more reasons to choose President University:

### a. English-Speaking University
The implementation of this benefit is ensured through the use of English as medium of instruction in all lectures carried out daily in President University. Students who might experience difficulties in carrying out this obligation are supported by the implementation of free English courses offered to all students for 6 semesters.

### b. International Environment
One of the reasons of its strong international environment is affected by the fact that President University is considered as the premier university in Indonesia with the highest enrolment of full-time international students. That said, local students have the opportunity to share classes and also connect with international students through various student organizations and events.

### c. Save Time
With the applicable curriculum, undergraduate students have the opportunity to finish their studies in just over 3 years.

### d. Portfolio Building Curriculum
Aside of the shorter study timeline, President University students are also given the chance to boost their portfolio as the application of curriculum that focuses on students’ practical experiences.

* **Year 1 portfolio:** participate in the Internship Experience program or Startup Building Program (Semesters 1 – 2), and get involved in Social Projects (Semester 3);
* **Year 2 portfolio:** decide on a concentration (available vary depending on the chosen study program) and Minor;
* **Year 3 portfolio:** pursue one of the Internship, Mobility, Scholar, or Startup Streams.

Aside of the experiences, students will also receive yearly resume reviews to elevate their portfolios.

### e. Value for Money
Taking into account the wide-range of scholarships offered in President University, access to higher education is possible for all prospective students who wish to further their studies.

## Academic Minor
Academic Minor is a learning program that may be taken by students from outside of their initial study program. Students planning to take this Academic Minor should report to the Head and Dean of their initial Study Program and Faculty. Additionally, students are also required to take all of the subjects determined to be the Minor subjects. These are required, so they can obtain the Academic Minor Degree acknowledgement.

The minimum credits to obtain the said degree acknowledgement may vary from one Study Program to another. This depends on what’s regulated under the Dean’s Decree.

### GPA Calculation

| No | Subject | Credits | Grade | Grade Number | Detail (Grd. num x Cr) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Course A | 3 | A | 4.00 | 12.00 |
| 2 | Course B | 6 | C | 2.00 | 12.00 |
| 3 | Course C | 1 | B- | 2.67 | 2.67 |
| | **Total Credits →** | **10** | | **Total grd. num x cr →** | **26.67** |

**Formula:**
$$GPA = \frac{\sum(Grade\ Number \times Credits)}{\sum Credits}$$

## English Development Programs

President University provides services to help students who wish to develop their English proficiency through the following bodies:
* English Language and Literacy Center / ELLC (for free services - ellc@president.ac.id);
* President Development Center / PDC (service fee applicable - pdc@president.ac.id).

In addition, we offer an exclusive program to help new students adjust to the English-speaking environment through the **English 0 Credits Program** (applicable for Batch 2022 onwards). This program consists of 6 progressive levels, conducted every term from Semester 1 to Semester 6. The course titles for each level are as follows:
* **Level 1**: Survival English
* **Level 2**: Fluency and Speed Development
* **Level 3**: Accuracy Development
* **Level 4**: English for Professional Purposes
* **Level 5**: Academic Writing
* **Level 6**: Research Writing

Active attendance and full participation in English 0 Credits classes are highly beneficial, as completion with a minimum grade of **B in each level** may fulfill the English Proficiency requirement for **Judicium** at the end of your studies. More details on this requirement are available in Rector’s Regulation Number 10 Year 2023 regarding the Minimum Standard of English Proficiency as a Graduation Requirement.

## Internship

All final-year students in President University will be provided an opportunity to experience direct exposure to professional and business atmosphere through the Internship program in various companies/ institution of their own choosing. Students will firstly be required to find an Internship placement. During the recruitment process, some companies/ institutions might require students to provide Internship recommendation letter. Students are encouraged to access the link to fill out the required fields in the Internship recommendation letter.

While carrying out this program, students will be closely supervised by both their on-site supervisor and internally assigned Internship advisor. This assessment will become a part of the students grading component. In addition to this assessment, students will be required to submit monthly progress reports to ensure the supervision function throughout. At the end of the program, students will also be required to submit a final report and presenting this report to the internal internship advisor. The final report will be assessed and graded by the advisor directly in the system. Therefore, attaching a wet signature on the report will not be necessary. More detailed explanation may be found on the Internship guideline provided by the ICC.

## Students and Lecturers’ Code of Ethics

To support the implementation of safe and peaceful teaching and learning environment, all students and lecturers’ activities are limited by the application of code of ethics. Please take notes on the below information regarding such limitations. In the event that you experience or witness the below acts, please notify the Ethics Board through email at: ethics@president.ac.id

### Students’ Code of Ethics

In compliance with Article 11 of the President University Rector’s Regulation Number 002/PR-R/III/KE.Mhs/2017/PresUniv regarding President University Students’ Code of Ethics, below are the acts prohibited under the above regulation:

a. Plagiarism, document forgery, and other types of fraudulence that is carried out either individually or in group;  
b. Disrespectful acts against a certain religion/belief that might be classified as blasphemy;  
c. Sexual violation including pornography, sexual harassment, and free sex within the University area;  
d. Violent crime, gambling, adultery, defamation, theft, fight, mental and physical abuse, distribution of illegal substances, and cyber crime;  
e. Carrying/using firearms and penetrating weapon within the University area;  
f. Possession, dealing, and use of narcotics, psychotropic, and alcohol;  
g. Destruction towards the University’s facility dan/or using the University’s facility without permission;  
h. Provocation that might interfere with the peace and the implementation of the University’s programs;  
i. Sexual harassment, rape, acts of prostitution (prostitutes and pimp), visualization of pornography in form of pictures, electronic media, and/or other documents;  
j. Wearing indecent clothing which might demonstrate harassment against a certain ethnicity, race, religion, and group;  
k. Other acts that violate and are prohibited under the prevailing laws and regulations of the Republic of Indonesia.

### Lecturers’ Code of Ethics

In compliance with Article 12 of President University Rector’s Regulation Number 001/PR-R/III/KE.Dosen/2017/PresUniv regarding President University Lecturers’ Code of Ethics and Article 1 and 2 of the Rector’s Regulation Number 6 Year 2020 regarding the Amendment of Rector’s Regulation Number 001/PR-R/III/KE.Dosen/2017/PresUniv regarding President University Lecturers’ Code of Ethics below are the acts prohibited under the above regulations:

a. Defamation of honor and reputation of the University;  
b. Failure to fulfill the Tri Dharma Perguruan Tinggi obligations assigned by the University;  
c. Acts that might incur losses to the University, Civitas Academica, Educational Staff, and Society;  
d. Provocation and destruction of harmony among group within the University;  
e. Abuse of power against students throughout both academic and non-academic activities;  
f. Leaking confidential information related to the University, Colleagues, Educational Staff, and Students;  
g. Distribution of false information regarding the University to Students, Colleagues, Educational Staff, and Society;  
h. Plagiarism and/or Auto-plagiarism in scientific research;  
i. Both physical and psychological sexual harassment carried out directly and/or indirectly against the students;  
j. Arbitrary and unfair acts towards Students, Colleagues, and Educational Staff in carrying out Tri Dharma Perguruan Tinggi obligations;  
k. Asking and/or receiving gifts from Students or other parties in relation to their authority and contradictory to their Tri Dharma Perguruan Tinggi obligations;  
l. Dating and/or having romantic relationship with students;  
m. Other acts that violate and contradict with the prevailing laws and regulations of the Republic of Indonesia;  
n. Attempting to get close to students on romantic purposes;  
o. Utters, writes, signals, or commits harsh/violent and unreasonable acts directly and/or indirectly to students.

## Streams

President University has designed an exclusive program for students that accommodates students' needs based on the mapping of their future career interests. These interests are divided into several categories depending on the prevailing curriculum (curriculum 2020 and 2022).

### Curriculum 2020

Curriculum 2020 currently prevails for **all students of batch 2020 – 2021** and **students batch 2022 from the Faculty of Business and Humanities**. Below is the detailed explanation on each of the Stream offered in curriculum 2020:

1. **Internship Stream** (coordinator: ICC - icc_pu@president.ac.id)

   Internships are planned and closely supervised "hands-on" work-related learning experiences providing the students with a chance to gain important knowledge and skills in a career-related that may or may not be directly related to their academic coursework. An internship provides exposure to career fields of businesses and interests.

   By putting the internship as the compulsory subject of the university (in curriculum 2020 and earlier), President University recognizes the importance of experiential learning opportunities for both the educational advancement of students and the growth of host employers' businesses and organizations.

   More detailed explanation may be found on the Internship guideline provided by the ICC.

2. **Start Up Stream** (coordinator: SetSail - setsail@president.ac.id)

   Start Up Stream is designed for students who have career interest in building their own business. Students who choose this Stream will be taught all the theories as well as practical experience to build and manage a business. By choosing this Stream, students are expected to gain the below skill set and competencies:

   a. Entrepreneurial Competence 1: Into Action
   
      This competency includes students' ability to:
      * Take initiative;
      * Plan and manage;
      * Cope with uncertaintym ambiguity, and risk;
      * Teamwork; and
      * Learn through experience.

   b. Entrepreneurial Competence 2: Managing Resources
   
      This competency includes students' ability to:
      * Mobilize others;
      * Financial & economic literacy;
      * Mobilize resources;
      * Motivation and perseverance; and
      * Self-awareness & efficacy.

   c. Entrepreneurial Competence 3: Idea and Opportunities
   
      This competency includes students' ability to:
      * Spot opportunities;
      * Creativity;
      * Vision;
      * Value ideas;
      * Ethical; and
      * Sustainable thinking.

3. **Scholarship Stream** (coordinator: International Office (for exchange) - international.office@president.ac.id / LRPM (for research assistant) – lrpmpu@president.ac.id / ICC (for teaching assistant) - icc_pu@president.ac.id)

   In compliance with the curriculum designed by all stakeholders in President University, it was decided that the Scholarship Stream is designed to accommodate students' interests in the following activities:

   a. Student Exchange
   
      By accommodating students' interests in student exchange activities, the University provides an equal opportunity for students to study in other Universities, be it nation-wide or overseas. There are two types of student exchange program that can be taken by the students:
      * Government based programs such as Indonesia International Student Mobility Awards (IISMA), *Beasiswa Transfer Kredit*, or *Pertukaran Mahasiswa Merdeka* (PMM); or
      * Partnership-based program with our partner universities such as University Utara Malaysia (UUM), Ritsumekan Asia Pacific University (APU), Tunghai University, National United University, and Laurea Applied University.

      To participate in the exchange programs offered by the Government, you are encouraged to read through the terms and conditions of each program through the Government's official websites. Meanwhile, all information regarding partnership-based student exchange program may be found on the President University's website.

      In general, only students in their second or third year who can participate in student exchange programs. In addition, those who plan to register in one of the above-mentioned program also need to obtain English proficiency certificate such as DET, IELTS, or TOEFL IBT and a GPA of >3.0.

      For more detailed information, students are encouraged to directly contact International Office (international.office@president.ac.id)

   b. Research Assistant
   
      Aside of hopping into the real academician world by volunteering to teach, students interested in becoming academicians could also prepare themselves earlier by becoming a Research Assistant. This option is too, as well as Teaching at School and Teaching assistant, accommodated under the Scholarship Stream. To take this option, students should:
      * Get a recommendation letter from the lecturer and be qualified to do research.
      * Find themselves an official lecturer to partner up with as students must be guided by at least one lecturer throughout the research process.
      * The research output target is an International Journal. Scopus indexed Q3 is the minimum standard for the said target.
      * Journal can be claimed to LRPM.

   c. Teaching at School or Teaching Assistant
   
      The followings are requirements for teaching assistant:
      
      **Student Eligibility**
      * Passed (A – C) 96 credits for Scholarship I or 114 for Scholarship II
      * Minimum TOEFL ITP of 550/ PET 550/ IELTS 6.5/ Duolingo 125
      * Minimum GPA 3.50
      * No retake subject(s) (has no D – E)
      * Recommendation letter/ approval email from the lecturer whom the student will assist
      
      **Lecturer Eligibility**
      * Minimum Lektor Kepala or currently serving in Structural Position (Rector/ Vice Rector/ Dean)
      * Minimum academic degree on par of S3 (Doctorate/ Ph.D.)

   In general, **students planning to take this Scholar Stream needs to obtain a minimum score of 550 in PET and Cumulative GPA of 3.5**. In addition, there are also several specific requirements that students need to take notes on:

   a. Requirements for research assistant:
      * Get a recommendation letter from the lecturer and be qualified to do research.
      * Find themselves an official lecturer to partner up with as students must be guided by at least one lecturer throughout the research process.
      * The research output target is an International Journal. Scopus indexed Q3 is the minimum standard for the said target.
      * Journal can be claimed to LRPM.

   b. Requirements for community service assistant:
      * Students who take community service must get a recommendation from the lecturer and be qualified to do community service.
      * Community service must be conducted together with the lecturer.
      * The community output minimum is Indexed Journal SINTA 3.
      * Journal can be claimed to LRPM.

4. **Mobility Stream** (coordinator: International Office - international.office@president.ac.id)

   By accommodating students' interests in student exchange activities, the University provides an equal opportunity for students to study in other Universities, be it nation-wide or overseas. There are two types of student exchange program that can be taken by the students:
   * Government based programs such as Indonesia International Student Mobility Awards (IISMA), *Beasiswa Transfer Kredit*, or *Pertukaran Mahasiswa Merdeka* (PMM); or
   * Partnership-based program with our partner universities such as University Utara Malaysia (UUM), Ritsumekan Asia Pacific University (APU), Tunghai University, National United University, and Laurea Applied University.

   To participate in the exchange programs offered by the Government, you are encouraged to read through the terms and conditions of each program through the Government's official websites. Meanwhile, all information regarding partnership-based student exchange program may be found on the President University's website.

   In general, only students in their second or third year who can participate in student exchange programs. In addition, those who plan to register in one of the above-mentioned program also need to obtain English proficiency certificate such as DET, IELTS, or TOEFL IBT and a GPA of >3.0.

   For more detailed information, students are encouraged to directly contact International Office (international.office@president.ac.id)

## Students' Code of Conduct

Students should always behave themselves in a manner that is polite, respectful, and responsible, and which conforms to the generally accepted rules of behavior, customs, and traditions of Indonesia.

### Dress and Appearance

* President University expects students to be modest and decent in dress and appearance.
* The University desires students to come to class neat and tidy, wearing appropriate and decent clothing.
* Inappropriate decoration is not suitable as general garb for attending classes.
* A clean and neat appearance and maintenance of personal hygiene is required on campus.
* Slippers or sandals are not allowed.
* Dress, shirts and pants should be properly ironed.

Students who do not conform to these expectations may be prohibited from attending classes.
